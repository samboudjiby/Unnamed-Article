The R95p index (Figure 4) shows a statistically significant increase (+5.78 mm/year).This implies that at Lake Guiers Basin either heavy rainfall events have become stronger, i.e., associated with a higher volume of rainfall each year, or they are becoming more frequent, or both. The R99p index shows the same R95p behavior, i.e. positive (1.70mm/year).
The RX5day and RX1day indicators that show the accumulated maximum rainfall in 1 day and in 5 days, respectively, are rising (2.87 mm/ day) and (1.57 mm/day) with statistical significance. In general, it is noted that the current rainfall climate positive trends are more marked.
Figure 5: Six months SPI in Lake Guiers Basin. Here, the 6-months SPI compares the precipitation for June-July-August-September-October-November with the same 6-months period over the historical record. A normal year is defined with an Index between -0.99 and 0.99, an Index ≥ 1 is classified as a wet year, while an Index of ≤ -1 is classified as a dry year.
The R25 mm indicator, illustrating the number of days in the year on which the total daily rainfall was above 25 mm, shows statistically significant positive trends (+0.14 day/year). This confirms that moderate to heavy rain events are becoming more frequent every year.
The CDD and CWD indices that illustrate the maximum number of consecutive dry and wet days, respectively, show negative trend (-0.84mm/day) and positive trend (0.077 mm/day) in the Basin.

As we said previously, precipitation is extremely variable in space and time, and future precipitation patterns are therefore more difficult to model and predict than temperature.
In regard to precipitation projection, most, but not all, of the AOGCMs available through Climate Wizard predict a significant decrease in annual precipitation in Lake Guiers Basin. Considering models ensemble average, a decreasing trend in noted is all scenarios for the rest of the century. This trend is more marked by end century in A1B scenario (-11.56%) and A2 (-15.43%).
However some models predict an increase in annual precipitation between 1 to 48% in B1 scenario and 3 to 17% in A2 scenario. For i-e while ECHAM5-MPI model project decreasing trends in precipitation up to (-23%) under A2 emissions scenarios by the end of the century, MRI-CGCM2.3.2 5 model project an increasing tend up to (10%).
