Beyond that, the region is affected by droughts since the 1970s. Their seasonal and episodic variation is due to the dependency on the monsoon. Arthurton et al. (2002) show that the isohyets in Senegal move southwards. While the isohyet of 400mm/a crossed the Lake Guiers region from 1931-60 it moved around 50km southwards in the observed period of 1961-90. Annual precipitation is consequently <400mm/year in Lake Guiers region and the Ferlo valley.

For the purposes of this study only three climatic factors temperature, precipitation and potential evaporation will be discussed in detail. Other factors, such as wind and humidity, are potentially important as well, but they are beyond the scope of this study.
